My Peptide Pal — Research Journal

My Peptide Pal is a private, on-device research journal designed for individuals tracking personal wellness protocols, daily wellbeing metrics, and body composition over time.

FEATURES

Cycle Tracker
Log and monitor research cycles from start date to end date. Record the compounds in each cycle, set duration, and track your progress day by day with a visual progress indicator. View active cycles at a glance on the home dashboard.

Daily Wellbeing Scores (1–10)
Log four daily scores — Energy, Sleep Quality, Mood, and Recovery — each rated 1 to 10. View 7-day trend charts. Scores are stored locally and can be exported to any AI app for personal analysis.

Daily Notes / Journal
Add free-text notes to each day. Record observations, how you feel, or anything relevant to your research.

Reconstitution Calculator
A mathematical tool that calculates concentration and draw volumes from vial size and water volume inputs. Results include ml draw volume and insulin syringe units. This is a mathematical reference tool only — it does not provide dosing advice.

Weight Log
Track body weight over time with kg/lbs support. View a 7-day trend chart with entry history.

Progress Photos
Capture and store progress photos privately on your device.

AI Export
Copy your cycle data to clipboard and open it directly in ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, or any AI app for personal research insights.

PDF Export
Export your full cycle report as a PDF for personal records.

PRIVACY & DATA

All data is stored exclusively on your device. Nothing is ever transmitted, uploaded, or shared. No account required. No sign-up. No cloud sync. Your data never leaves your phone.

To delete all data: More → Delete All Data.

FREE & PRIVATE

My Peptide Pal is completely free. There are no subscriptions, no ads, and no data collection.

DISCLAIMER

My Peptide Pal is a personal research journal and tracking tool. It is not a medical device and does not provide medical advice, dosing recommendations, or treatment guidance. Always consult a qualified healthcare professional before making any changes to your health regimen.
